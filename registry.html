Okay, look, people, here’s the deal. We’re in our 40s. We’re not setting up a house. We have everything we need, and an awful lot of the things we want. We are very, very lucky to be where we are in life, and asking for more just seems gross. So we haven’t registered anywhere.

Instead, if you feel moved to make a gift, we would be deeply honored and grateful if you would make it in the form of a donation to a cause that we value. Below are links to some of the organizations that are meaningful to us and that we support. Please choose whichever ones speak to you, and consider that our registry.

Recipe collection

We are asking for one thing, though. One of Rebecca’s hopes and dreams would be to receive one or two favorite recipes from each guest that could start her new family cookbook. Please consider emailing or snail-mailing* your recipe to Rebecca, or bringing it to the wedding. It can be for the stove, oven, grill, slow cooker, Instant Pot, or convection oven — she has all the gadgets! (See what we mean?)

Places to donate (thank you!)

  • ACLU
  • Animal Rescue League of Boston: 118-year-old shelter providing adoption and rescue services, as well as animal cruelty investigations and advocacy for improved animal protection laws
  • Black Lives Matter
  • Doctors Without Borders
  • Electronic Frontier Foundation: defends digital privacy, free speech, and innovation
  • Environmental Voter Project: a very interesting organization that uses measurable, proven techniques to increase active voting participation by environmentalist voters (founded and run by a friend)
  • Farm Sanctuary: shelters rescued farm animals and promotes laws and policies that support animal welfare and animal protection through rescue, education, and advocacy
  • Food for Free: a local organization that rescues food from going to waste and directs it to people in need
  • Hispanic Federation’s Puerto Rico hurricane relief fund
  • Innocence Project: exonerates the wrongly convicted through DNA testing and reforms the criminal justice system to prevent future injustices
  • NARAL
  • National Immigration Law Center
  • Natural Resources Defense Council
  • Pine Street Inn: a Boston homeless shelter and provider of housing and job training services
  • Planned Parenthood
  • Puppies Behind Bars: trains prison inmates to raise service dogs for wounded war veterans, and explosive-detection canines for law enforcement
  • Rosie’s Place: a Boston women’s shelter and provider of services and advocacy
  • Southern Poverty Law Center
